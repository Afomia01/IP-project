Harar Jugol - Old City

Welcome to Harar Jugol, the old walled city in Ethiopia with a rich history and cultural significance. Here are some key highlights:

History

Harar Jugol, founded in the 7th century, is considered one of the holiest cities in Islam. It has served as a major center for trade, scholarship, and cultural exchange over the centuries. Harar was part of the Harla Kingdom's domain in the sixth century.


Architecture

The city is known for its unique architecture, characterized by narrow winding alleys, traditional Harari houses with vibrant colors, and numerous mosques. The Jugol is surrounded by an ancient wall. he traditional Harari house design is still widely in use today, with only minor changes, and Hararis who have moved to other cities try to adhere to a similar style Hararis take great pride in their houses, and they form an important part of Harari culture


Cultural Heritage

Harar Jugol is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized for its cultural and historical significance. It has a diverse population and is known for its vibrant markets, traditional coffee houses, and religious celebrations.


Demographics

Based on the 2007 national census conducted by the Central Statistical Agency of Ethiopia (CSA), Harar city had a total urban population of 99,368, of whom 49,727 were men and 49,641 were women The plurality of urban inhabitants professed Ethiopian Orthodox Christianity, with 48.54% of the population having reported they practiced that belief, while 44.56% of the population said they were Muslim, and 6.14% were Protestant

Walls and gates of Harar

The Old City of Harar with an approximate area of 60 hectares (150 acres) is surrounded by ramparts on the northern and eastern sides and part of the southern side. There are five ancient city gates, These are, clockwise from the north of the citadel:

  • Assum Bari (located in the north)
  • Argobba Bari (located in the east)
  • Sukutat Bari (located in the south east)
  • Badro Bari (located in the south)
  • Asmadin Bari (located in the west)
